Hopper started with a simple observation: most budgeting apps feel punishing. They show you what you spent and make you feel bad about it. I had a 100+ day on Duolingo but couldn't stick to a budget for a single week. Duolingo works because it made me want to come back. What if a budgeting app felt like that?

I submitted Hopper as my entry to the Girls Who Code AI4FinancialGood competition, where it placed in the top 6 out of over 150 submissions.

## The Concept

Hopper uses AI to analyze a user's financial data and generate a personal, adaptable budget plan each month. Rather than presenting a static budget, it breaks long-term financial goals into daily completable actions that feel achievable. Saving $500 in a month may seem unachievable, but Hopper presents goals as actionable breaking $500 down into small tasks like "skip the Starbucks, bring a coffee from home instead."

Completing goals earns Lotus Flowers, an in-app currency used to unlock character cosmetics. The reward loop is intentional: external motivation (customization) bridges the gap until the habit of saving becomes intrinsic.

A personalized chatbot provides financial guidance scoped to each user's specific situation. Not generic advice, but responses grounded in their actual spending and goals.

Every month, Hopper initiates a short survey to re-evaluate priorities and adjust the budget plan for the coming month, keeping the experience adaptive rather than set-and-forget.

## Design

I designed the full app wireframes in Figma, working through the onboarding flow, home dashboard, goal-tracking screens, and the chatbot interface. The visual language borrows Duolingo's friendliness, placing a friendly frog as the mascot of the app.
