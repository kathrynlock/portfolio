End-to-end UX case study for UT Austin's campus resource navigation. Started with a broad discovery phase — 30 student interviews surfaced that most people couldn't find the services they needed within 3 clicks. Iterated through 5 rounds of usability testing and landed on an information architecture that cut average task completion time by 40%.

Key decisions: flattening the nav hierarchy, adding a persistent search bar, and building a personalization layer that remembers your most-visited resources. Shipped to a pilot group of 200 students; full rollout planned for fall semester.
