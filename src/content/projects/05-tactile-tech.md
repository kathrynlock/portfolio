Independent study project exploring tangible user interfaces — hardware and software that you interact with by touching physical objects rather than a screen. Built three prototypes using Arduino: a data sculpture that represents air quality as physical texture, a knob-based music interface, and a pressure-sensitive drawing tool.

The research component surveyed 18 participants on their emotional response to tangible vs. screen-based interaction. Tangible interfaces consistently scored higher on "feels calm" and "feels in control" — findings that align with the broader Analog Revival research I was doing in parallel.
