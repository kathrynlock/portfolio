I keep a Note on my phone titled "Sweet Treat Survey" and it currently has 47 entries. I am moving it here so my friends will stop texting me for recommendations and so I can finally retire the screenshot.

The methodology: I rank by croissant first (it is the canonical pastry and reveals everything), then by atmosphere, then by whether I would walk more than 15 minutes to go back. Items marked with a star have caused me to actually reschedule plans to return.

Top tier, no debate: the kouign-amann at a place on South Congress that does not have great signage and is easy to miss. I walked past it four times before someone finally pointed at it. The lamination is what you want lamination to be.

Strong second tier: the cardamom roll from the weekend pop-up at the farmers market. Not always available. Worth checking. Also the olive oil cake from the Italian place near campus — technically a cafe not a bakery but I am making an exception.

The place on Rainey Street has beautiful looking pastries that do not quite deliver on the promise. I include it because the coffee is excellent and sometimes you want atmosphere more than a perfect croissant.

Still investigating: I have been told there is a Taiwanese bakery somewhere in north Austin with milk bread worth driving for. I have not found it yet. When I do, this list will be updated.

Last updated: December 2025. This list is never finished.
