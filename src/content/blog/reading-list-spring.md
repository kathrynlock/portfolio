I keep a seasonal reading list mostly so I can prove to myself, at the end of the year, that I read more than I think I did. Here is the spring batch, with notes.

The best thing I read: a novel recommended by a professor that I kept putting off because the title seemed pretentious. I was wrong. It was one of those books that makes you feel slightly altered afterward, in the way good books sometimes do. I have since pressed it on four people.

The novella I cannot stop recommending: very short, extremely strange, about a woman who starts receiving letters from her past self. I do not want to say more because the setup sounds like a gimmick and it is not. If you trust me at all, just read it.

The essay collection I abandoned halfway through: I am including this because I think it is honest to mention the things that did not land. The writing was good but the subject matter left me cold, and I finally gave myself permission to stop. Life is short and the to-read pile is taller than my desk lamp.

The poetry: I committed to one collection per month this year. Spring's entry was a debut collection by someone I had never heard of, picked off a best-of list. The third poem made me read it out loud to my roommate at 9am on a Saturday. She was very patient about it.

Still on the list for summer: a biography I have been meaning to read for two years, a design history book I bought impulsively, and whatever my book club picks, which I will read in forty-eight hours the weekend before the meeting.
