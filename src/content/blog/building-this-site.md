I rebuilt this site four times before I let myself ship it. Here is what each version got wrong, what the final one finally got right, and the strange satisfaction of a paper-grain SVG noise filter at 6% opacity.

Version one was a Notion page I exported and called a portfolio. I am not counting it. Version two was a template I broke trying to make it mine. Version three was almost good but the typography felt like it belonged to someone else.

The fourth version started with color. I had a notebook spread where I had swatched every pastel I owned and tried to find the ones that felt like a place I would actually want to spend time. Lavender, mint, warm cream, a dusty rose. Not a palette so much as a feeling.

The thing that took the longest was the paper texture. I wanted the kind of warmth you get from actual cardstock without it looking like a design cliché. SVG feTurbulence filters can give you that if you tune them carefully — opacity matters more than you expect, and 6% is the number I kept coming back to.

The fonts came next: a serif for display, another for body text, a monospace for dates and labels. Choosing type is the part of design that feels most like listening. You are trying to hear what the page wants to say and pick the voice that fits.

I also made a tweaks panel — a hidden toolbar that lets me adjust things like the hero size and card style without touching code every time I want to try something. It turned into a useful habit: make the thing configurable, then configure it until it feels done.

What I learned from building it four times is that a portfolio is a working document. It is not supposed to be finished. The best version of it is the one that still has room to grow into.
