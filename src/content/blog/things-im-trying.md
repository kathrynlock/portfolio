I do not really do resolutions. But I do keep a running list of small experiments — things I would like to try for a month or a week or just once — and I revisit it every few months to see what stuck.

This year's list, in no particular order: writing one letter per week (ongoing, going well), not checking my phone for the first thirty minutes of the day (ongoing, going inconsistently), trying a new recipe every two weeks (ongoing, currently stuck in a risotto phase), reading one book of poetry per month (ongoing, this one is easy).

Some things that already dropped off: meditating every morning (lasted six weeks, replaced by morning walks which feel equivalent), journaling every night (replaced by journaling when I actually have something to say, which is better), cold showers (I was never going to do this one honestly).

The thing I have learned from keeping these lists is that the experiments that stick are the ones that replace something you were already doing, or slip into a gap in your day without requiring extra willpower. Cold showers require willpower. Morning walks just require walking.

The most surprising one that stuck: writing down one good thing at the end of each day. Not gratitude journaling in the heavy sense — just a single sentence, on a sticky note, about one thing that was good. The stack of sticky notes on my desk is currently about two inches thick.

I will report back in six months. Some of these will have stuck. Some will have become different experiments. At least one will have become something I do automatically and no longer think of as an experiment, which is the best possible outcome.
